Life Right Now - 7/18/2020

July 18, 2020 in Personal

As promised, here's my first weekly update. Even though I claimed in my justification for the weekly model that it would allow me to come up with better content, it turns out that, well, it did not. I just got lazier.1 Instead of some interesting post about a deep topic, here's what's happened in the last week:

  • Summer CS has been mostly fine. There are two weeks left and we're just about to start the fifth unit.2
  • I've been putting off watching the series finale of Avatar: The Last Airbender until this weekend.3
  • Shameless plug: HackTJ 7.5 is November 21-22, 2020.4 Registration opens September 25th at 8pm and closes on October 16th. Mark your calendars, because you don't want to miss it!
  • I spent hours playing the Wikipedia game (you know, the one where you try to get from one page to another through only hyperlinks) in the name of research. We love TJ Science Olympiad.5
  • My sister thinks I'm addicted to Civilization VI. As someone who spends much of her free time on Roblox,6 I'm sure she would know about being hooked on video games.
  • I saw an image of a fancy building in Vienna, Austria earlier, and now I am listening to Richard Strauss's Wiener Philharmoniker Fanfare on loop.7

  1. Laziness is like entropy: it takes work to reduce within the system, but the universe is gaining more of it and there's nothing you can do. ↩︎

  2. That's a bit concerning, because I only did the first five units, so what comes after we finish unit 5? The answer is work a final project. Blech. ↩︎

  3. It's probably not going to happen at this rate. I watched it shortly after writing this post. ↩︎

  4. And it's virtual! Wow! So cool! ↩︎

  5. To be fair, we were intending on testing its viability as something to do for group hangouts. Our conclusion: it works, though Firefox users might have some issues with the site we were using. ↩︎

  6. Let's just pretend she's not quietly reading a book as I write this post. ↩︎

  7. Subsequent research has revealed that I am absolutely terrible at distinguishing Vienna's various palaces. Apparently it's the St. Michael's Wing of the Hofburg Palace. Also, who needs that many palaces in one city? ↩︎
